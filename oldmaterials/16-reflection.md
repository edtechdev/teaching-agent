**Module 4: Wellness as Foundation**

You built a personal wellness plan.

### Weeks 5-9: Skills

**Module 5: How You Learn Best**
You went deep on six evidence-based learning strategies: active recall, spacing, elaboration, interleaving, dual coding, and retrieval practice. You stopped studying the way everyone else does and started studying *how your brain works best.*

**Module 6: Taking Effective Notes**
You learned that notes aren't transcriptions. They're *thinking tools.* You explored six different systems and found one that fit you.

You learned the power of review.

**Module 7: Time Management & Productivity**
You did a time audit and got real about how you spend your days. You explored four different frameworks. You addressed procrastination honestly—the root causes, not just the symptoms.

You learned to manage yourself, not just your time.

**Module 8: Test Preparation & Resilience**
You stopped seeing tests as judgment and started seeing them as information. You learned a strategic preparation timeline. You built a resilience framework for bouncing back from disappointment.

You learned that struggle doesn't define you.

**Module 9: Midterm Reflection**
Right in the middle, you stopped and looked back. You celebrated what you'd learned in the previous eight weeks. You reflected on how you've grown.

You created a personal learning narrative—your story of becoming.

### Weeks 10-15: Applied Planning

**Module 10: Understanding Your Academic Path**

You learned that your education is something you *design*, not something that just happens.

**Module 11: Building Your Education Plan**

You connected your education to your purpose.

**Module 12: Career Development & Exploration**

**Module 13: Career Goals Presentation**

**Module 14: Financial Literacy & Planning**

**Module 15: Engaging in Community**

## Takeaways to Carry Forward

As you move into the remainder of your college experience (or into your next chapter), carry these with you:

### 1. Your Brain Can Change

Neuroplasticity isn't just a concept. *You've experienced it.* You've learned things you didn't know before. You've developed skills. Your brain rewires through practice and repetition.

**Going forward:** Never accept "I'm not good at this" as final. With the right strategies and effort, you can learn.

### 2. The Way You Study Matters More Than How Long

You don't need to study longer. You need to study smarter.

**Going forward:** Use spacing, active recall, elaboration. Stop passive review. Study the way your brain learns best.

### 3. Wellness Isn't Separate from Academic Success

You can't study your way out of sleep deprivation. You can't willpower through burnout.

**Going forward:** Protect sleep. Move your body. Eat real food. Build community. These aren't distractions—they're the foundation.

### 4. Your Purpose Matters

When you're exhausted or struggling, your purpose is what keeps you going.

**Going forward:** Return to your purpose statement regularly. When you're choosing between commitments, does one align better with your purpose? Trust that direction.

### 5. Help-Seeking Is Strength

Every high-performing person uses support. They know who to ask. They ask when they're stuck.

**Going forward:** Don't wait until you're drowning. Ask early. Ask often. Know your support systems.

### 6. Financial Reality Is Part of the Equation

You can't ignore money because it makes you uncomfortable. You have to understand it, manage it, and make intentional choices about it.

**Going forward:** Keep building financial literacy. Understand your debt. Make informed decisions about your future. This directly impacts your options.

### 7. Your Community Carries You

You can't do college (or life) alone.

**Going forward:** Keep showing up for your people. Invest in real friendships. Also BE there for others. Reciprocal community is powerful.

### 8. Resilience is Built, Not Born

You're not born resilient. You build it by:
- Trying hard things
- Failing and getting back up
- Asking for help
- Adjusting your approach
- Celebrating incremental progress

**Going forward:** When you face difficulty, remember: you've bounced back before. You can do it again.

### 9. Your Career Isn't Set in Stone

That career direction you created? It's real *right now*. But you'll learn new things. You'll discover new interests. You'll gain new information.

**Going forward:** Stay open. Keep exploring. Adjust your direction as you learn. This isn't failure—it's responsiveness to new information.

### 10. You're Capable of More Than You Think

This is the meta-lesson.

You came into this course (this semester, this college experience) with limitations and doubts. *And you're still here.* You've built plans. Created strategies. Asked for help. Connected with community.

**Going forward:** Believe in your capacity to grow. You've proven it to yourself.
