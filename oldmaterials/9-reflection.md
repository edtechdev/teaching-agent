## Reflecting on Growth

Growth isn't always obvious in the moment. You have to stop and look back to see it.

### Module 1: How You Learn & Growth Mindset

Think back to the beginning of this course.

**Then:** You might have had beliefs like "I'm just not good at math" or "I'm a bad test-taker" or "I'm not a morning person so I can't study well."

**Now:** You understand that your brain is plastic. You can develop any skill with practice. You've probably already seen this in action—a skill that felt impossible at the beginning of the semester starting to feel manageable.

**Reflection questions:**
- What's one belief you had about your learning ability that's shifted?
- What evidence do you have of growth?
- How have you applied growth mindset thinking in an actual class or situation?

### Module 2: Your Purpose Statement

Think about your purpose statement. You wrote it weeks ago.

**Then:** You might have felt uncertain about your direction or unsure what you actually wanted.

**Now:** You've had time to sit with it, revise it, live it. Does it still feel true? Have you deepened your understanding?

**Reflection questions:**
- Read your purpose statement again. Does it still feel authentic?
- What's shifted in how you see your academic, career, or personal goals?
- How have your choices in the past weeks aligned with this statement (or not)?

### Module 3: Support Systems

Think about reaching out for help.

**Then:** Maybe you didn't know where support lived or felt nervous about asking.

**Now:** You've mapped resources. Maybe you've actually used one (visited your advisor, went to tutoring, talked to a counselor). You know where to go.

**Reflection questions:**
- Have you used any support systems?
- If yes: How did it go? How could you use them more?
- If no: What's holding you back? What small step could you take?

### Module 4: Mental Health & Wellness

Think about your foundation.

**Then:** You were establishing baseline practices. Maybe sleep was erratic, stress management was nonexistent, community felt far away.

**Now:** You've had weeks to notice what helps. You've probably experimented with at least one wellness practice.

**Reflection questions:**
- What's one wellness practice you've actually been able to maintain?
- What's still a challenge?
- What would help you build sustainable wellness habits?

### Module 5: How You Study

Think about your study approach.

**Then:** You might have been using whatever study method you'd always used, without question.

**Now:** You understand evidence-based strategies. You've probably tried at least one evidence-based approach and noticed a difference.

**Reflection questions:**
- Which evidence-based strategy (active recall, spaced repetition, elaboration, etc.) has been most helpful for you?
- How have your study results shifted compared to past semesters?
- What study approach feels most aligned with your learning style?

### Module 6: Note-Taking

Think about how you capture information.

**Then:** Your note-taking might have felt messy, disorganized, or unhelpful.

**Now:** You've tried a new system or refined your current approach. You understand the review step. You're probably taking better notes.

**Reflection questions:**
- Which note-taking system did you try?
- Has your note-taking improved?
- How has better note-taking affected your understanding and retention?

### Module 7: Time Management

Think about how you spend your hours.

**Then:** You might have felt busy and didn't know where your time was going or why.

**Now:** You've done a time audit. You understand your priorities. You're probably clearer about where your time actually goes.

**Reflection questions:**
- What surprised you in your time audit?
- Are you currently using a time management system?
- Has your sense of control over your time improved?

### Module 8: Test Preparation & Resilience

Think about how you approach exams.

**Then:** You might have been cramming or getting anxious or struggling with bouncing back from low grades.

**Now:** You have concrete strategies for exam prep. You understand anxiety management. You have a framework for resilience.

**Reflection questions:**
- Have you used your new exam preparation strategy?
- Has test anxiety shifted?
- If you've struggled academically, how have you bounced back?

## The Bigger Picture: Integration

You don't learn these skills in isolation. They all work together.

**Good sleep** (Module 4) helps you focus when studying (Module 5).

**Knowing your priorities** (Module 7) helps you say yes to what matters (Module 2—your purpose) and no to what doesn't.

**Good note-taking** (Module 6) combined with **good study strategies** (Module 5) means less time needed to learn (Module 7).

**Using support systems** (Module 3) helps you manage stress (Module 4) and bounce back from academic challenges (Module 8).

This is why we did these modules in this order. Each one builds on the previous.

## Challenges & Next Steps

You've also probably hit some challenges. That's expected. That's growth.

**Common challenges at the midpoint:**

**"I know the strategies but don't do them"**
- Knowing is just the first step. Doing requires practice and reminders.
- Suggestion: Pick one practice and commit to it for 2 weeks. Make it non-negotiable.

**"I tried something and it didn't work"**
- Not every strategy works for everyone.
- Suggestion: Try a different strategy. Get feedback from others. Adjust.

**"Things are still hard"**
- College is hard. These new strategies make it more manageable, not effortless.
- Suggestion: Be patient with yourself. Building habits takes time.

**"I'm doing better but don't see it"**
- Growth is incremental. You might not see it day-to-day.
- Suggestion: Compare where you are now to where you were at the start of this course.

**"I've been inconsistent"**
- That's normal. Humans aren't robots.
- Suggestion: Instead of all-or-nothing, focus on consistency. If you miss a day, start again tomorrow.
