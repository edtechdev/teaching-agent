## Course Overview

### Course Title
**College Success: Academic Skills, Planning & Intentional Living**

### Target Audience
Undergraduate students (first-year to senior year) seeking to:
- Develop practical academic success skills
- Understand how they learn best
- Build resilience and growth mindset
- Plan their education intentionally
- Explore career options
- Manage finances responsibly
- Find community and belonging

### Course Format
- **Duration:** 16 weeks (asynchronous, self-paced)
- **Time Commitment:** 3-5 hours per week (66-82 hours total)
- **Delivery:** Online learning management system (Blackboard, Canvas, Moodle, etc.)
- **Prerequisites:** None (designed for any student at any stage)
- **Cost:** Free (no additional materials required beyond college resources)

### Course Philosophy

**College Success** is grounded in three core beliefs:

1. **Everyone Can Succeed** — Success in college is not about innate talent; it's about strategy, support, and growth.

2. **Wellness Enables Learning** — Mental health, sleep, nutrition, stress management, and community are not separate from academic success; they're the foundation.

3. **Planning with Purpose Matters** — When students understand their purpose and align their education with their values, they make better choices and stay more engaged.

The course employs a **supportive learning coach** approach. You (the instructor) are positioned as a caring guide who believes in every student's potential and provides practical strategies and resources for success.

## Learning Outcomes

Upon completion, students will be able to:

1. **Create a personal purpose statement** detailing their academic, career, and personal goals and alignment
2. **Create a presentation** detailing their career goals and lifestyle expectations
3. **Design an education plan** aligned with their academic, career, and personal goals
4. **Identify and discuss college support systems** available to enhance academic success
5. **Engage and document participation** in college initiatives and community life
6. **Demonstrate financial awareness** for college and personal success
7. **Develop academic skills** that facilitate success as a student
8. **Maintain personal and mental health and wellness**

### Setting Up in Your LMS

1. **Create Course Shell**
   - Title: College Success
   - Duration: 16 weeks (or customize for your calendar)
   - Visibility: Set to visible when ready

2. **Create Module/Week Folders**
   Create folders in your LMS matching this structure:

     - Capstone portfolio: 15%
     - Engagement/completion: 10%

7. **Add Resources/Links**
   - Create a "Resources" page with:
     - Link to your campus counseling services
     - Link to your writing center

     - Link to career services
     - Link to financial aid office

## Assessment & Grading

### Three Major Projects

#### Project 1: Personal Purpose Statement (Module 2)
**Learning Outcome:** LO #1 — Create a personal purpose statement detailing academic, career, and personal goals and alignment

**Deliverable:** 2-4 page written statement including:
- Personal, academic, and career values
- Vision for college experience
- Alignment statement connecting values to goals

**Suggested Grading Rubric:**
- Clarity & Completeness (25%): All three goal areas addressed clearly
- Values Integration (25%): Demonstrates understanding of personal values
- Alignment (25%): Shows how goals connect and reinforce each other
- Reflection Depth (15%): Evidence of genuine self-reflection
- Writing Quality (10%): Clear, organized, grammatically sound

**Feedback Focus:** Celebrate the self-reflection. Offer questions that deepen thinking if clarification is needed.

---

#### Project 2: Education Plan (Module 11)
**Learning Outcome:** LO #3 — Design an education plan aligned with academic, career, and personal goals

**Deliverable:** 2-4 page written plan including:
- Academic goal/degree information
- Degree requirements summary
- Semester-by-semester course plan (remaining semesters)
- Special opportunities (internships, research, study abroad)
- Connection to purpose statement and career goals

**Suggested Grading Rubric:**
- Completeness (30%): All required sections present, detailed course plan
- Accuracy (20%): Accurate degree requirements and sequencing
- Feasibility (20%): Plan is realistic and balanced
- Integration (20%): Connects to purpose and career direction
- Clarity (10%): Easy to understand and follow plan

**Feedback Focus:** Validate the strategic thinking. Offer suggestions for optimization if you see opportunities. Encourage consultation with academic advisor.

---

#### Project 3: Career Goals Presentation (Module 13)
**Learning Outcome:** LO #2 — Create a presentation detailing career goals and lifestyle expectations

**Deliverable:** Choose one format (students choose):
- Written Career Goals Statement (2-3 pages)
- Video Presentation (3-5 minutes)
- Slide Presentation (10-15 slides)
- Interview-Format Recording (recorded video answers to interview questions)

**Content Requirements:**
- Career direction (specific but flexible)
- Why it matters (values + passion)
- Lifestyle & work environment preferences
- How student is building toward this
- Openness to change/evolution

**Suggested Grading Rubric:**
- Career Direction Clarity (25%): Specific, well-researched, realistic
- Values Connection (25%): Clear connection to personal values and purpose
- Authenticity (20%): Sounds like the student, genuine
- Building Strategies (20%): Clear plan for developing relevant skills/experience
- Format Quality (10%): Professional presentation in chosen format

**Feedback Focus:** Celebrate the career exploration. Validate the direction even if it's exploratory. Ask questions that deepen thinking about next steps.

---

#### Capstone Project: College Success Portfolio (Module 16)
**Learning Outcome:** All 8 LOs — Integration and reflection

**Deliverable:** Comprehensive portfolio including:
- Personal College Success Narrative (4-6 pages) — Where I started → My journey → Where I am now
- Personal Purpose Statement (from Module 2)
- Education Plan (from Module 11)
- Career Goals Presentation (from Module 13)
- One evidence of growth (study system, wellness plan, financial plan, etc.)
- Next Steps & Future Orientation (1 page)

**Suggested Grading Rubric:**
- Narrative Quality (25%): Coherent story of growth; well-written
- Integration (25%): Connects all projects and learning into cohesive vision
- Reflection Depth (20%): Genuine metacognitive awareness
- Evidence Quality (15%): Artifact of growth is meaningful and well-explained
- Future Orientation (15%): Clear next steps and commitments

**Feedback Focus:** This is a celebration. Affirm the growth. Acknowledge the work. Send students off with genuine encouragement.

### Participation & Engagement

Beyond the three major projects, students engage through:

**Discussion Participation (20-25% of grade)**
- Each module has 2-3 discussion prompts
- Students should post original responses AND respond to peers
- Suggested: 1-2 posts per week, at least 2 peer responses per week
- Focus on quality of thinking, not length

**Reflection Completion (5-10% of grade)**
- Each module has individual reflection questions
- These may or may not be collected/graded—your choice
- If graded, focus on completion and evidence of thinking, not "right answer"
- Many instructors use these as ungraded self-assessment

**Module Engagement (5-10%)**
- Did student read/engage with module content?
- Attendance at any optional synchronous sessions?
- Responsiveness when reaching out with support?

## Common Questions & Answers

### How long does this course take to teach?
**Answer:** Preparation time varies by how much you customize. First time: 6-10 hours to load into LMS and customize resources. Ongoing teaching: 5-8 hours per week for 16 weeks (discussion responses, project feedback). If co-teaching or having a TA help, time can be shared.

### Can I shorten this from 16 weeks?
**Answer:** Technically yes, but not recommended. The course is designed with careful pacing so content isn't rushed and projects get time for revision. If you need to condense, I'd recommend 12 weeks minimum, and you'd need to reduce or combine some modules. Contact for guidance on streamlined versions.

### Can I use just parts of this course?
**Answer:** Yes! The course is modular. You could teach just the skills block (Modules 5-9) as a standalone study skills course. Or just the planning block (Modules 10-16) as a major/career planning course. Each module is self-contained enough to work independently, though connections enrich the experience.

### What if my students are online and asynchronous?
**Answer:** This course is DESIGNED for asynchronous online delivery. No synchronous meetings required. Discussion forums, email, announcements, and office hours solve any need for real-time interaction. Perfect for your context.

### What if some students join late?
**Answer:** Each module is self-contained, so students can join late and jump into the current module. For projects, you can extend deadlines or let them do projects on their schedule. The course is forgiving of variable pacing.

### How do I know if students are actually learning?
**Answer:** Multiple signals: discussion post quality, project deliverables, completion of reflections, engagement patterns. The three major projects are your strongest evidence of learning outcomes. Consider a 1-minute survey mid-course asking what they're learning.

### What's the attendance/participation policy?
**Answer:** That's up to you and your institution. Suggested: Discussion participation grade (20-25%). Suggested: All three major projects required (can't pass without them). Suggested: Completion of action plans/reflections encouraged but maybe not graded.

### How do I handle students with accommodations?
**Answer:** College Success Fundamentals is excellent for all learners:
- Asynchronous = flexible timing for medical, mental health, work needs
- Multiple engagement modes = accommodates different learning styles
- Discussion = accessible to deaf/hard of hearing if captioned; captions generally available in most LMS
- Projects offer format flexibility (written, video, presentation)
- Module content is text-based and can be adapted for screen readers
- Consider adding transcripts if you include videos

Ask your disability services office how to best support your students' needs.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Issue: Low Discussion Participation
**Possible Causes:** Prompts feel boring, students feel unsafe sharing, unclear expectations, no modeling from instructor

**Solutions:**
- Post a response to discussion prompts yourself, modeling depth
- Be the first to share vulnerability ("Here's what I struggled with...")
- Make expectations crystal clear (1 original post + 2 responses = full credit)
- Send friendly reminder mid-week if participation is light
- Consider grading discussion to signal importance

### Issue: Projects Submitted Late
**Possible Causes:** Unclear deadlines, students underestimate time, competing priorities, procrastination, life events

**Solutions:**
- Make deadlines VERY visible (syllabus, module, week view, reminder email)
- Breakdowns dates (e.g., "Draft due Week 11, final due Week 12")
- Allow revision on projects (encourages submission)
- Check in mid-project for progress
- Understand life happens—be flexible within reason
- Consider "late submission with 10% penalty" rather than 0 credit

### Issue: Students Not Engaging with Modules
**Possible Causes:** Too much content, not understanding why it matters, technical issues, depression/mental health, other demands

**Solutions:**
- Check technical access—can they actually access? Do links work?
- Reach out personally: "I noticed you haven't started—anything I can help with?"
- Refer to counseling if you suspect mental health impact
- Break modules into smaller chunks if they feel overwhelming
- Have students set one small goal per week
- Celebrate small wins ("You completed the reflection—great start!")

### Issue: Diverse Participation Levels
**Possible Causes:** Different learning paces are normal; some students are very engaged, others minimally so

**Solutions:**
- This is normal and okay—not everyone engages the same way
- Highly engaged students can deepen thinking through discussion
- Minimally engaged students might be learning silently (reading, reflecting privately)
- Focus on completion of required work, not uniform engagement style
- Reach out if someone goes silent for extended time (2+ weeks)

## Tips for Instructor Success

### Mindset Shifts

1. **You're a Coach, Not an Authority**
   - Position yourself as guide, not expert-delivering-truth
   - Model vulnerability ("Here's what I struggle with too...")
   - Ask questions more than give answers

2. **You Can't Save Everyone**
   - Some students won't fully engage—that's okay
   - You can offer support; they have to accept it
   - Focus on what you can control: being available, clear, supportive

3. **Attendance ≠ Learning**
   - Students reading silently, reflecting, thinking = learning
   - Not all learning shows up in discussion or projects
   - This is asynchronous; pace varies

### Practical Tips

- **Set Office Hours & Stick to Them** — Consistency helps students trust you
- **Respond Within 48 Hours to Email** — Shows you care and are present
- **Grade Projects Generously on First Draft** — Focus on feedback, not punishment
- **Celebrate Progress Explicitly** — Call out growth when you see it
- **Use Your Own Struggle as Teaching** — Show how YOU use these strategies
- **Connect Students to Campus Resources** — Know what's available; use it
- **Protect Your Own Wellness** — You can't pour from empty cup; set boundaries
- **Save Good Student Work (with permission)** — Use examples in future semesters
