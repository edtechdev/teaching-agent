# Course Outline

## Title

College Success

## Target Audience

First-year college students beginning their college journey who want to build the skills, habits, and mindset needed to thrive academically and personally.

## Time Commitment

Estimated 3 hours per module (self-paced; total course time depends on the number of modules completed)

## Abstract

**College Success** is a self-paced course designed to help first-year college students build the skills, habits, and mindset they need to succeed — not just in their courses, but in life beyond graduation.

Through engaging, evidence-based modules, you will explore how learning actually works, develop a growth mindset, and practice proven study techniques including time management, effective note-taking, and smart test preparation. You'll also navigate the human side of college: managing stress, supporting your mental and physical health, and knowing when — and how — to ask for help.

Beyond the classroom, this course helps you explore the bigger picture: clarifying your values and purpose, researching career paths, choosing and planning your major, and making informed financial decisions. By the end of this course, you'll have a concrete academic plan and a full toolkit of evidence-based strategies to carry you through college — and beyond.

**Topics covered include:** how learning works, growth mindset, study skills, time management, note-taking, help-seeking, academic planning, academic and social support, mental health, physical health, career development, and financial literacy.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you will be able to:

1. **Study smarter:** Apply evidence-based study strategies — including time management, note-taking, and learning science principles — to improve academic performance in your courses
2. **Build resilience:** Use social-emotional learning strategies to manage stress, regulate emotions, and sustain motivation throughout college
3. **Know yourself:** Identify your personal values, strengths, and goals to guide your academic and life decisions
4. **Plan your future:** Research career interests and explore majors, then create a concrete academic plan aligned with your goals
5. **Seek support:** Proactively reach out to academic, social, and mental health support systems when you need help — and know how to do it effectively
6. **Manage your money:** Develop foundational financial literacy skills to make informed decisions that support your long-term success

> **Note:** This outline includes 6 learning objectives (the recommended range is 3–5). Consider consolidating objectives 3 and 4 in a future revision if you want a tighter scope.
