# Course Agenda

## Overview

This self-paced agenda for **College Success** is designed to help you build momentum one module at a time.

Each module is planned for about **3 hours** and follows the same learning flow from the didactic concept:

1. Engage
2. Learn
3. Reflect
4. Apply
5. Check

The sequence starts with core academic success skills (how learning works, study methods, and time systems), then moves into resilience and support habits, and finishes with long-range planning (purpose, career/major direction, and financial literacy).

By the end, you will have both immediate tactics for this semester and a practical plan for your next steps.

## Modules / Sessions

### Module 1

- **Title:** How Learning Works and Why Mindset Matters
- **Estimated duration:** 3 hours
- **Type:** lecture
- **Learning objective(s):** Apply learning science principles and growth mindset strategies to improve study effectiveness
- **Summary:** You will learn how memory, attention, and practice actually work, then use that knowledge to replace unproductive habits with strategies that improve retention and performance.
- **Materials file:** `materials/1-lecture.md`

### Module 2

- **Title:** Time Management and Focus Systems
- **Estimated duration:** 3 hours
- **Type:** exercise
- **Learning objective(s):** Use practical time planning and focus routines to manage coursework and reduce overload
- **Summary:** You will build a realistic weekly plan, identify time leaks, and practice simple routines that protect focus and prevent last-minute stress.
- **Materials file:** `materials/2-exercise.md`

### Module 3

- **Title:** Note-Taking and Study Strategies That Actually Work
- **Estimated duration:** 3 hours
- **Type:** exercise
- **Learning objective(s):** Apply evidence-based note-taking and study techniques to improve grades
- **Summary:** You will compare common study habits against proven methods, then practice retrieval, spacing, and note-taking systems you can use in your current classes.
- **Materials file:** `materials/3-exercise.md`

### Module 4

- **Title:** Stress, Resilience, and Healthy Performance
- **Estimated duration:** 3 hours
- **Type:** lecture
- **Learning objective(s):** Use social-emotional learning strategies to regulate stress and sustain motivation
- **Summary:** You will learn how stress affects learning, build a personal reset plan, and choose daily habits that support your mental and physical wellbeing.
- **Materials file:** `materials/4-lecture.md`

### Module 5

- **Title:** Help-Seeking and Building Your Support Network
- **Estimated duration:** 3 hours
- **Type:** exercise
- **Learning objective(s):** Proactively seek academic, social, and wellbeing support when needed
- **Summary:** You will map your campus support system, practice asking for help effectively, and create a step-by-step plan for what to do when challenges appear.
- **Materials file:** `materials/5-exercise.md`

### Module 6

- **Title:** Values, Purpose, and Goal Setting
- **Estimated duration:** 3 hours
- **Type:** lecture
- **Learning objective(s):** Identify personal values, strengths, and goals to guide academic decisions
- **Summary:** You will clarify what matters to you, connect your values to your college choices, and turn broad ambitions into concrete short-term goals.
- **Materials file:** `materials/6-lecture.md`

### Module 7

- **Title:** Career Exploration, Major Choice, and Academic Planning
- **Estimated duration:** 3 hours
- **Type:** exercise
- **Learning objective(s):** Research career interests and majors, then build a practical academic plan
- **Summary:** You will use simple career exploration tools, evaluate major options, and draft an academic plan aligned with your goals and timeline.
- **Materials file:** `materials/7-exercise.md`

### Module 8

- **Title:** Financial Literacy for College Success
- **Estimated duration:** 3 hours
- **Type:** lecture
- **Learning objective(s):** Apply foundational financial literacy concepts to make informed college decisions
- **Summary:** You will learn core budgeting, aid, debt, and cost-planning concepts so your financial choices support your academic success and long-term stability.
- **Materials file:** `materials/8-lecture.md`
